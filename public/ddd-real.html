Catatonia is a neurological disease that affects how people process and react to their surroundings. People with catatonia frequently do not react to what is going on around them or respond in unexpected ways. Catatonia has been investigated by researchers since 1874, yet it remains mostly undiagnosed since it was previously thought to occur mainly in patients with schizophrenia. Additional diagnostic issues include disagreements within psychiatry over how many and which criteria are required to identify catatonia
