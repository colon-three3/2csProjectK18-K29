Exploding Head Syndrome

or EHS

Exploding Head Syndrome (EHS) is a sleep disorder defined by the sense of sudden, loud noises or an exploding sensation in the head while falling asleep or waking up. EHS episodes generally occur during the transition from wakefulness to sleep. People with EHS report hearing sudden, loud noises. These sounds appear to originate from within the skull rather than from outside sources. The episodes are brief and painless, although they can be unpleasant and cause sleep difficulties. Some people report seeing flashes of light, experiencing electric shock, or brief immobility comparable to sleep paralysis. Despite the name, EHS is not actually lethal.
